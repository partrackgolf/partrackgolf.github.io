Frequently Asked Questions

How do I scan a scorecard?

On the iPhone, tap New Round → Scan Scorecard. The camera opens automatically. Hold the card flat in good light and let auto-shutter capture it. ParTrack pulls out the pars and per-tee yardages on its own. Review the result, fix anything that's off, and save it to your library.

How does sync work between iPhone and Apple Watch?

ParTrack uses Apple's WatchConnectivity framework to mirror courses, favorites, round history, and your active round between your iPhone and paired Apple Watch automatically. Start a round on one device, finish it on the other — strokes update live on both.

Why doesn't my handicap show up?

ParTrack calculates your World Handicap System (WHS) index from 18-hole rounds played on courses that have a tee with a course rating and slope. You need at least 3 complete 18-hole rounds with rated tees before an index is shown. Nine-hole rounds are saved to history but don't enter the handicap calculation.

Can I play a 9-hole round?

Yes — on the iPhone, choose Front 9 or Back 9 at round setup. The watch defaults to 18-hole rounds; if you want to play 9 on the watch, start the round from the phone first. Nine-hole rounds save to history but don't count toward your handicap index.

Where is my data stored?

All your courses, rounds, and history live on your device. There are no servers and no accounts. If you have a paired Apple Watch, the same data is mirrored there via Apple's sync framework. Uninstalling the app removes all local data.

How do I delete a saved course or round?

On the iPhone, swipe left on any row in My Courses or Round History to reveal a delete button. Watch is read-only for the course library — to remove a course, do it from the phone.

The scorecard scan didn't capture the pars correctly. What can I do?

Tap the back chevron on the result screen to re-launch the scanner. Try to hold the card flat, fill the frame, and avoid glare. If a particular cell stays wrong, tap it on the result screen to edit the value by hand before saving.

Does ParTrack show GPS, course maps, or distances?

No. ParTrack is intentionally focused on stroke counting and handicap tracking. GPS, distances, and course maps are common in other golf apps — ParTrack stays out of that space so the experience stays simple and the battery stays alive.
